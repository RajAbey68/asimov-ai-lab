# The Asimov-AI Methodology

> **Canonical Reference** — All website copy, assessment tools, and consulting materials must align with this document.

## Positioning Statement

> The Asimov-AI methodology evaluates artificial intelligence systems through the lens of legal, regulatory, ethical, reputational, intellectual property, and security risk, using established governance frameworks and emerging laws such as the EU AI Act as the benchmark for readiness and prioritisation.

## Core Philosophy

Most organisations deploy AI before they understand its risks. The Asimov-AI framework reverses the traditional approach — instead of asking *"How do we build AI?"* it asks *"How do we deploy AI responsibly and defensibly?"*

## The Six Risk Domains

| # | Domain | Focus |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | **Legal Risk** | Decision accountability, liability exposure, explainability, contractual implications |
| 2 | **Regulatory Risk** | EU AI Act classification, governance documentation, transparency obligations, human oversight |
| 3 | **Ethical Risk** | Dataset bias, algorithmic fairness, transparency to users, oversight of automated decisions |
| 4 | **Reputational Risk** | Brand exposure, public perception, client trust, governance transparency |
| 5 | **IP Risk** | Training data ownership, dataset licensing, model provenance, copyright risks |
| 6 | **Security Risk** | Model manipulation, prompt injection, data leakage, access control |

## Framework Alignment

The methodology does not invent new risk doctrine. It applies existing legal and governance frameworks:

- **EU AI Act** — Risk-based classification of AI systems
- **NIST AI RMF** — AI Risk Management Framework
- **ISO/IEC 42001** — AI governance standard
- **ISACA COBIT** — Governance and risk management
- **GDPR** — Data protection and privacy

## The AI Risk Scorecard

The entry-point diagnostic. Organisations complete a structured assessment across the six risk domains.

**Output includes:**
- AI risk maturity score
- Risk breakdown by domain
- Priority remediation actions
- Framework alignment gaps

**Format:** 5–15 minutes, forming the entry point into deeper advisory engagement.

## The Advisory-to-Delivery Bridge

```
AI Risk Assessment → Governance Readiness → Practical Deployment
```

Asimov-AI is positioned as both:
1. **Trusted internal adviser** — governance, risk, executive oversight
2. **Pragmatic implementation partner** — automation, workflow deployment
